What we do

Measuring what volcanoes breathe

NOVAC instruments — scattered across crater rims, hillsides, and remote outposts — scan the sky for sulfur dioxide (SO₂) and other volcanic gases around the clock. Each measurement is a data point in a picture of volcanic activity that no single observatory could ever capture alone.

Why it matters

From crater to decision

Volcanic gas emissions are one of the earliest warning signs of unrest. NOVAC data helps scientists and civil authorities make faster, better-informed decisions — protecting millions of people living near active volcanoes.

🌋

Eruption forecasting

Rising SO₂ flux often precedes eruptions. Continuous monitoring helps observatories spot the signal before it becomes a crisis.

🌍

Climate science

Volcanoes inject gases into the stratosphere that affect global temperatures. NOVAC data constrains these inputs in climate models.

✈️

Aviation safety

Ash and gas clouds are hazards to aircraft. Real-time emissions data supports air traffic decisions during volcanic crises.

📡

Open science

NOVAC data and software are openly available — enabling researchers worldwide to build on a shared foundation.

Who we are

Scientists, engineers, and observers

NOVAC is sustained by volcano observatories and research institutions that share instruments, software, and expertise. Membership spans every active volcanic region on the planet — from Iceland to Indonesia, from Colombia to Japan.
